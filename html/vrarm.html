VR Tracking SystemFor the human arm

Year 3 Project

For behavioural researchers, VR technology represents a powerful tool to examine human behaviour in three-dimensional computer-generated environments. However, the lack of low-cost wearables allowing full limb tracking restricts the scope of the possible studies that would benefit from the versatility of VR platforms.

My 3rd Year University Project consisted in designing a low-cost wearable device enabling full arm tracking in a VR throwing experiment.

Mechatronics and Robotics (Beng) Year 3 Project: Design of an Embedded Motion-Tracking System for the Human Arm

Final Grade: First Class
