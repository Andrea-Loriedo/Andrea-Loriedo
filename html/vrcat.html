VR CognitiveAssessment Tool

Internship Project at ICON

A tool to easily run and manage VR sensorimotor experiments, powered by Unity. Designed and developed over two consecutive summers at the Immersive Cognition Laboratory (ICON) in Leeds School of Psychology.

Inspired by the original, tablet-based Clinical Kinematic Assessment Tool, VRCAT was commissioned by ICON to bring the scalable assessment of Fundamental Motor Skills into three-dimensions. Allowing for 3D positional and rotational tracking, VRCAT can record movement data for the head and hands with all 6 degrees of freedom. Powered by the Unity Experiment Framework, it's flexible structure facilitates the collection of data across multiple experiments, such as the Aiming, Tracking and Interceptive Timing tasks that were developed and integrated into the battery.
